CLIDE: Real or Fake?

Abstract

We present CLIDE, a zero-shot method for detecting AI-generated images using a conditional likelihood approximation over CLIP embeddings. Our approach applies a whitening transform to image features and uses the resulting approximate Gaussian likelihood as a detection score—without training on generated images. CLIDE supports both general and domain-specific settings and is designed to remain effective under practical distribution shifts across generators and domains.

Key Insights

Conditional Likelihood (Zero-shot)

Detect fakes via an approximate likelihood score derived from CLIP embeddings—no classifier training on generated images required.

Whitening as a Likelihood Surrogate

Whitening makes the embedding space closer to Gaussian, enabling stable likelihood estimation and improved shift robustness.

Domain-specific Adaptation

Use domain-representative sets to specialize statistics, improving performance on practical target domains (e.g., damaged cars, invoices).

Practical Deployment

Simple pipeline: embed → whiten → score. Works as a drop-in detector alongside existing vision systems.
